Evaluate and Communicate Business Requirements Should Know

The main focus areas for the BA's requirements communications activities are as follows:

  • Create a requirements communications plan.
  • Manage requirements conflicts.
  • Determine requirements format.
  • Create a requirements package.
  • Conduct a requirements presentation.
  • Conduct a requirements review.
  • Obtain requirements signoff.

What He should consider the following issues when designing a communications plan?

    The BA will develop a framework to document the timing, method, and stakeholders involved with the requirements.

  • Information that needs to be shared and how it will be delivered.
  • Who is the appropriate recipient for a knowledge set?
  • When does information need to be delivered?
  • Stakeholder authority level (sign off, input delivered, or review only).
  • Requirements that have been elicited high level versus detailed, business versus end.
  • Communication of final deliverables.

Starting a Project

A requirements communication plan indicates the BA's intentions regarding the delivery of information to stakeholders. It is a document that establishes:

  • The objectives the BA seeks for the communications process (for instance ensure complete stakeholder understanding of the requirements).
  • The ways in which the objectives will be accomplished (that is, defining the process).
  • The audience to whom communications will be delivered.
  • The tools and timetable for delivery of information.
  • The measurements to be used to evaluate the plan's results.
